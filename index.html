La Carrera Panamericana
Autonomous Drive

La carrera panamericana autonomous drive is the first experimental project for autonomous vehicles on public roads in Mexico,encompassing a wide variety of routes and real-world driving conditions.
Designed and developed by Tecnológico de Monterrey in collaboration with Politecnico di Milano.
Phase 1
Route selection for La Carrera Panamericana 2025 in mixed mode (Autonomous and manual).
Phase 2
A six-month experimentation phase to parameterize the technology within the operational domain, addressing a wide range of extreme contexts throughout the entire route.
Phase 3
To undertake La Carrera Panamericana 2025 route in mixed mode.

The Localization Module
Determines the vehicle's position on the defined trajectory using data from GPS, IMU, RTK, and LiDAR. It ensures precise navigation by fusing sensor data.

The Perception Module
Processes sensor data to identify the position, speed, and type of surrounding objects. This includes recognizing vehicles, pedestrians, and static elements like road signs.

The Planning Algorithm
The usage of advanced algorithms to ensure a safe and optimal path. Defines the ideal trajectory and recalculates it dynamically when obstacles are detected.

The Vehicle Control Module
It manages acceleration, braking, and steering.
